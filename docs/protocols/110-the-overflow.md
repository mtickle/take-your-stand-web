The first protocols were about defense—shields, walls, and armor to keep you safe in the storm. But we were not created just to survive; we were created to sing.

The "Overflow Protocol" is the shift from defense to offense. It is based on the biblical principle that gratitude is not just a polite reaction to a blessing; it is a spiritual weapon. When Paul and Silas sang in prison, the earth shook and the doors flew open. When King Jehoshaphat sent the choir out to the front lines of the battle, the enemy armies turned on each other.

The enemy can handle your worry, he can handle your planning, and he can even handle your theology. But he cannot handle your praise. This protocol is designed to turn your gratitude into a lifestyle that keeps the door open for God’s power to move. We are going out in glory.

## 1. The Memorial Stone

> “In the future, when your children ask you, ‘What do these stones mean?’ tell them that the flow of the Jordan was cut off before the ark of the covenant of the Lord. When it crossed the Jordan, the waters of the Jordan were cut off. These stones are to be a memorial to the people of Israel forever.” (Joshua 4:6-7)

Humans suffer from chronic spiritual amnesia. God can part the Red Sea on Tuesday, and by Friday we are worried about water. When God stopped the Jordan River, Joshua didn't just say "cool"; he commanded the men to haul twelve massive stones out of the riverbed to build a monument. Why? Because he knew they would forget. We must physicalize our gratitude. Unless you write it down or mark the moment, the miracle will fade into the background noise of life.

:::info[Rejoice]

- 📡 **Indicator:** A breakthrough, an answered prayer, or a moment of clear provision.
- ⚡ **Action:** Create a physical "Ebenezer." Write the date and the victory on a rock, put a note in a jar, or frame a memento.
- 🎯 **Goal:** Solidify the memory to prevent future spiritual amnesia.
  :::

## 2. The "Undignified" Praise

> “Wearing a linen ephod, David was dancing before the Lord with all his might, while he and all Israel were bringing up the ark of the Lord with shouts and the sound of trumpets. As the ark of the Lord was entering the City of David, Michal, daughter of Saul, watched from a window. And when she saw King David leaping and dancing before the Lord, she despised him in her heart.” (2 Samuel 6:14-16)

King David knew that you cannot worship God while trying to look cool. True gratitude requires the death of your dignity. When he brought the Ark home, he didn't care about his royal robes or the opinions of the onlookers; he cared about the presence of God. If you only worship when it is polite and comfortable, you are worshipping your own reputation. Sometimes, you have to break the jar of perfume. You have to lift your hands, raise your voice, and let the joy out.

:::info[Rejoice]

- 📡 **Indicator:** A swell of inner joy or a realization of God's goodness.
- ⚡ **Action:** Express it physically. Sing loud in the car, lift your hands in church, or kneel in your living room. Do not contain it.
- 🎯 **Goal:** Let inner gratitude become outer expression.
  :::

## 3. The "Habit-Stack" Offering

> “Rejoice always, pray continually, give thanks in all circumstances; for this is God’s will for you in Christ Jesus.” (1 Thessalonians 5:16-18)

How do you "pray continually"? You can't stay on your knees 24 hours a day. The secret is "Habit Stacking"—attaching a spiritual discipline to a physical habit. Gratitude should not be an event you visit; it should be the atmosphere you live in. If you drink coffee every morning, let that first sip be a trigger for a "Thank You." If you drive to work, let the ignition turning over be a trigger for praise.

:::info[Rejoice]

- 📡 **Indicator:** Mundane daily tasks (pouring coffee, starting the car).
- ⚡ **Action:** Attach a specific "thank you" to the habit.
- 🎯 **Goal:** Weave gratitude into the fabric of your ordinary day.
  :::

## 4. The "Prodigal Party"

> “Bring the fattened calf and kill it. Let’s have a feast and celebrate. For this son of mine was dead and is alive again; he was lost and is found.” (Luke 15:23-24)

Religion is often somber, but the Kingdom of God is a party. When the Prodigal Son returned, the Father didn't lecture him; He threw a barbecue. God loves to celebrate restoration. We are often too stingy with our joy. When God provides a victory or a new beginning, do not just nod your head. Celebrate it. Eat the good food. Invite the friends. Laughter and community are forms of spiritual resilience because they declare that the enemy did not win.

:::info[Rejoice]

- 📡 **Indicator:** A milestone of grace, a new beginning, or a season of restoration.
- ⚡ **Action:** Host a real celebration. Gather your people, share a meal, and verbally give glory to God for what He has done.
- 🎯 **Goal:** Reflect the joyful, celebratory heart of the Father.
  :::

## 5. Pray

_"Father, I am running over! You have turned my mourning into dancing and my sorrow into joy. I refuse to be silent about Your goodness. Thank You for the battles You have won for me, and thank You for the peace that guards my heart. I praise You not just for what You do, but for who You are. You are Good. You are Faithful. You are Here. Let my life be a living song of gratitude. To You be the glory, forever and ever. Amen."_
