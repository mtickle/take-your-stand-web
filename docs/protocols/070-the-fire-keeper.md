> "The fire on the altar must be kept burning; it must not go out. Every morning the priest is to add firewood and arrange the burnt offering on the fire and burn the fat of the fellowship offerings on it. The fire must be kept burning on the altar continuously; it must not go out.” (Leviticus 6:12-13)

There are times when we are not fighting a specific enemy, but simply fighting entropy. You feel dry, distant, or "out of sync" with God. Your prayers feel like they are hitting the ceiling, and reading the Bible feels like reading a tax manual.

We often interpret this distance as God leaving us. We think, "God, where did You go?"

_But God is omnipresent; He does not move. If the distance has grown, it is because we have drifted._ Spiritual fire is like physical fire: it requires fuel, oxygen, and attention. If you stop adding wood, the fire goes out. This protocol is not about emotional hype; it is about the deliberate mechanics of re-ignition.

## 1. The Ash Removal

> “If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness.” (1 John 1:9)

In the Old Testament temple, the first duty of the priest was to remove the ashes from the previous day's fire. Why? Because old ash chokes a new flame. Sometimes we feel a deficit because we have allowed "micro-sins"—resentment, cynicism, or small compromises—to pile up. They aren't "big" sins, but they are thick enough to block the airflow of the Spirit.

:::info[Take Your Stand]

- 📡 **Indicator:** Feeling spiritually "clogged" or unable to sense God's presence.
- ⚡ **Action:** Run a 60-second sweep. Seek anything that might be between you and God. Confess the small things immediately. Clear the ash so the fire can breathe.
- 🎯 **Goal:** Restore the airflow of the Spirit by removing the clutter of unconfessed sin.
  :::

## 2. The Fuel Source

> “When your words came, I ate them; they were my joy and my heart’s delight, for I bear your name, Lord God Almighty.” (Jeremiah 15:16)

You cannot warm yourself by a fire you didn't build. We often try to run on "spiritual fumes" from a sermon we heard three weeks ago. That is not enough. Fire requires fresh wood daily. You don't read Scripture to "finish a chapter"; you read it to find one stick of wood—one truth—to throw on the fire of your heart for the day.

:::info[Take Your Stand]

- 📡 **Indicator:** Feeling empty or burning out.
- ⚡ **Action:** Find that single verse. Stop trying to read whole chapters if you are dry. Find one verse. Meditate on it. Consume it until you feel your spirit strengthen.
- 🎯 **Goal:** Quality over quantity. One log burns better than a pile of wet leaves.
  :::

## 3. The Signal Restoration

> “Be still, and know that I am God.” (Psalm 46:10)

In a world of constant noise, our spiritual signal often gets jammed. We are so over-stimulated by phones, news, and podcasts that we lose our sensitivity to the Holy Spirit. A spiritual deficit is often just a symptom of too much noise.

:::info[Take Your Stand]

- 📡 **Indicator:** Feeling scattered, anxious, and unable to focus on prayer.
- ⚡ **Action:** Take a 10-minute digital fast. Phone in the other room. No music. Just silence. Tell God, "I am here, and my mind is quiet." (Again, reference the clarity of my “library moment”).
- 🎯 **Goal:** Lower the noise floor so you can tune out the world's static and rest firmly in His presence and truth.
  :::

## 4. The "First Works" (Revelation 2:4-5)

> “Yet I hold this against you: You have forsaken the love you had at first. Consider how far you have fallen! Repent and do the things you did at first. If you do not repent, I will come to you and remove your lampstand from its place.” (Revelation 2:4-5)

When Jesus addressed the church that had lost its passion, He didn't tell them to feel better; He told them to do better. He said, "Do the things you did at first." Feelings follow motion. If you don't "feel" in love with God, do the things you did when you were in love. Worship. Give. Serve. The feeling will catch up to the feet.

:::info[Take Your Stand]

- 📡 **Indicator:** Waiting to "feel" spiritual before you act spiritual.
- ⚡ **Action:** Pick obedience over emotion. Do one act of worship or service today, even if you feel dry. Ignite the fire by friction if necessary.
- 🎯 **Goal:** Jumpstart the heart through the action of the hands.
  :::

## 5. Pray

_"Lord, I confess that my fire has burned low. I have been distracted, busy, and operating in my own strength. I am tired of running on empty. Today, I clear the ash of my distraction. I bring the fresh wood of Your Word to my heart. I quiet the noise of the world to hear Your voice. Fan into flame the gift of God within me. I am ready to burn again. Amen."_
